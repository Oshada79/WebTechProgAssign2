The Sea Turtle Family

Five Species Of Sea Turtles In Sri Lanka


♥Five of the world's seven sea turtle species are found in Sri Lanka.

♥The Green, Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles are among them.


I.The Green Sea Turtle

The Green turtle is the most commonly sighted sea turtle in Sri Lanka. Also know as the green turtle,BLACK TURTLE or PACIFIC GREEN TURTLE. Is a species of large sea turtle of the family Cheloniidae.They are named after their green-coloured fat, and they can weigh up to 200 kilograms. The Green turtle may be found in various places of Sri Lanka, including the southern coast, the eastern coast, and the west coast. They are herbivores and mainly feed on sea grass and algae. During the nesting season, female Green turtles come ashore to lay their eggs on the beaches.

II.The Olive Ridley Sea Turtle

The Olive Ridley turtle is the smallest of the sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. Also known commonly as the PACIFIC RIDLEY SEA TURTLE. Is a species of turtle in the family Cheloniidae.They are named after their olive-coloured shell and can weigh up to 50 kilograms. The Olive Ridley turtle is a vulnerable species due to their nesting behaviour. Unlike the Green turtle, Olive Ridley turtles nest in large groups known as arribadas.Olive Ridley Turtles nest yearly and many lay their eggs on Kosgoda Beach, depositing more than 150 at one time. They are omnivores, eating crustaceans, fish and some marine vegetation. This critically endangered turtle is the largest of the 5 species and is a rare find in Sri Lanka.

III.The Loggerhead Sea Turtle

The Loggerhead sea turtle is a species of oceanic turtle distributed throughout the world.The Loggerhead turtle is a large sea turtle weighing up to 280 kilograms. They are named after their large head, which is shaped like a log.These marine reptiles are found mostly in salt water sometimes hundreds of miles out in the ocean and also in estuarine habitats such as bays, lagoons and salt marshes with females coming ashore momentarily for nesting. Being carnivores it feeds mainly on marine invertebrates, especially hard shelled crustaceans, which it devours with its powerful jaws. It is believed that the Loggerhead Sea turtle’s lifespan is approximately 50 years.

IV.The Hawksbill Sea Turtle

The Hawksbill turtle is a critically endangered species in Sri Lanka due to their high demand for their beautiful shell. They are named after their narrow and pointed beak, which resembles a hawk’s beak. Hawksbill turtles can weigh up to 80 kilograms and are found in many parts of Sri Lanka, including the southern coast, the eastern coast, and the west coast. Its habitat is mainly coral reefs and shallow lagoons where it feeds on sponges, fish, sea anemones and jellyfish amongst others

V.The Leatherback Sea Turtle

The Leatherback Sea turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is regarded as the fourth-heaviest modern reptile and the largest living turtle.They are named after their leathery skin, unique among sea turtles. It may be identified by its big, teardrop-shaped body that is a massive shade of grayish-black and is coated in skin and greasy flesh, as well as by the seven distinct ridges that run along its body from front to back. The leatherback sea turtle can reach heights of up to seven feet (two meters) and can weigh over two thousand pounds (900 kilograms). It can swim swiftly through the water thanks to its hydrodynamic body structure and a sizable pair of front flippers because it primarily feeds on jellyfish and other small marine invertebrates. Leatherback turtles are rare in Sri Lanka and are mainly found in the island’s northern waters.
